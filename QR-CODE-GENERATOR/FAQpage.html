Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about QuickQR

QuickQR is a free, unlimited QR code generator that allows you to create QR codes for various types of content without any restrictions. Simply enter your URL, text, email, phone number, SMS, or Wi-Fi credentials, click "Generate QR Code," and download your custom QR code instantly.

Our service supports multiple formats including PNG, JPG, SVG, and PDF, making it easy to use your QR codes across different platforms and media.

Yes! QuickQR is completely free with no hidden fees, no registration required, and no limits on the number of QR codes you can generate. We believe QR code generation should be accessible to everyone.

QuickQR supports multiple QR code types:

  • URL: Website links and web pages
  • Text: Plain text messages
  • Email: Email addresses with optional subject and body
  • Phone: Phone numbers for direct calling
  • SMS: Pre-filled text messages
  • Wi-Fi: Network credentials for easy connection
  • PDF: Document links and file sharing

QuickQR offers multiple download formats to suit your needs:

  • PNG: High-quality raster image, perfect for web and print
  • JPG: Compressed format for smaller file sizes
  • SVG: Vector format that scales without quality loss
  • PDF: Document format ideal for professional printing

Creating a Wi-Fi QR code is simple:

  • Select the "Wi-Fi" option from the tabs
  • Enter your network name (SSID)
  • Choose your security type (WPA/WPA2, WEP, or None)
  • Enter your network password
  • Click "Generate QR Code"

When someone scans the QR code, their device will automatically connect to your Wi-Fi network without manually entering credentials.

Yes, your privacy is our priority. QuickQR processes QR code generation entirely on the client-side, meaning your data never leaves your browser. We don't store, track, or have access to the content you encode in your QR codes.

However, remember that QR codes themselves are not encrypted - anyone who scans your QR code will be able to see the encoded information.

QR codes have inherent limitations based on the type of data:

  • Numeric data: Up to 7,089 characters
  • Alphanumeric data: Up to 4,296 characters
  • Binary data: Up to 2,953 bytes
  • Kanji characters: Up to 1,817 characters

For URLs, we recommend keeping them under 2,000 characters for optimal scanning performance.

Most modern smartphones can scan QR codes natively:

  • iPhone: Open the Camera app and point it at the QR code
  • Android: Open the Camera app or use Google Lens
  • Dedicated apps: Download a QR code scanner from your app store

The device will automatically detect the QR code and display the encoded information or take the appropriate action.

If your QR code isn't scanning well, try these solutions:

  • Ensure good lighting when scanning
  • Hold the camera steady and at an appropriate distance
  • Check that the QR code isn't damaged or distorted
  • Try a different QR code scanner app
  • For very long URLs, consider using a URL shortener first
  • Ensure the printed QR code has sufficient contrast

QR codes themselves never expire - they're just a way to encode information. However, the content they point to might become unavailable:

  • URLs might lead to pages that are removed or moved
  • Phone numbers might be disconnected
  • Email addresses might be deactivated

The QR code will continue to work as long as the underlying content remains accessible.
